Cécile Renouard analyses the adaptation needs of our policies and the need for collaborations between living people, drawing on the phenomenon of megafires.

"The house burns and we look elsewhere"  These comments
President Chirac at the Johannesburg Summit
foot of the letter, when pest fires multiply, including
the forest of Fontainebleau, close to the estate of Campus of Transition, through a France subjected to heat waves
successively. We also remember the huge fires that
devastated Canada in the summer of 2023, destroying millions
of hectares of forest equivalent to the area of Japan.

Give room for robustness, not performance

Scientists warn us that we need to move on
of a probabilistic mode of reasoning to the consideration of
uncertainty and the permanent possibility of the disaster.
The question is how to make us capable of us.
adapting to various crisis situations, without limiting ourselves to
programmable linear temperature evolution logics or
degradation of the living. This involves changing software, and
to recognize the bankruptcy of a method of reasoning based on the
control of clearly identified and separated phenomena
Other. Changing logic is, in the terms of the ecologist
Olivier Hamant, replacing robustness with performance, ensuring
the stability and viability of humans and living environments despite
large fluctuations. Recognize interdependencies and
Vulnerabilities, and seek to act at the root of problems:
We must both invoke for our political societies a
duty to mitigate - that is to say energy output
fossil fuels, decarbonization of our production methods,
moving, heating, feeding... and a duty
adaptation (or adaptability) to chaotic situations,
that is to ensure access to goods and
basic services for a decent life. You can see it.
disaster will be even stronger and
that we do not take the path of sobriety, of the
"prosperity without growth"
density growth based on fossil energy extraction
and non-renewable resources); and each disaster is
the more strongly felt as the means of dealing with it
were not anticipated.

Megafires, symptoms and symbols of unpreparation

Public policy preparation is key:
as regards fires, the book published by Joëlle
Zask in 2019, When the forest burns , is a summer reading
exciting applied political philosophy. The thesis of the
philosopher is to show how this phenomenon of megafoils
90% of which have a human origin and 40% are criminal, is a fact
total socio-natural, which says complicity between on one side
and the logic of compartmentalization of knowledge and
practices that cause global warming, and
the other, the means to fight the fires. On
often individualizes responsibilities, including
of arsonists or firefighters : but the megafires are for one part the
result of refusal to accept brulis, directed lights,
"domesticated" in a sense, as they
was practiced in history, and recently again by the
Aboriginal peoples. The author puts particular emphasis on the
Australian concept of "cleaning country",
a form of "care" of the earth:
The development of the territories goes through the fires.

The mutual care of our common world

This vision is quite close to that carried by philosophers
American living, Emerson and Thoreau, from a care to be cultivated
of nature, as well as
of anthropocentrism and the concern for the preservation of
Wild. Thus it is a matter of pleading for dialogue between natures
related to various cultures. To be able to find ways of
to combat present and future disasters, it is urgent
value the complexity, diversity of policy instruments,
dialogue between residents, users, foresters, firefighters, etc. of
feed a truly democratic approach, in line with John
Dewey on ways to consider community from
the 'great society'. In this sense, according to Joëlle Zask, the approach
American border can be revisited as
openness to novelty and unforeseeability and be related to
the notion of the horizon to care for, through the landscapes.

Finally, in view of the increase in the number and frequency of
megafires, facing the abominations generated by the fire that destroys
all, it is a question of becoming aware of the instrumentalisation
possible fire in a pyro-terrorism logic, tool
and demotivation of populations with
then lost their heritage and horizon.

In
dark contexts, make the wish that collective lucidity
greater on the roots of mega-fires and our incuries, we
allow us to better prepare ourselves for the unedited, by mutual care
of our common world. Cécile
Renouard
