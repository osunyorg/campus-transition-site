Join us as a volunteer
To experience an immersion at the Transition Campus in volunteering, please send us an application email.

Apply to become a volunteer
https://framaforms.org/benevolat-campus-de-la-transition-1741280465


To accomplish our mission of training and welcoming groups in immersion in the eco-location, volunteer support is invaluable. We therefore offer volunteer missions to assist us, for regular ad hoc missions or for immersion trips. An opportunity to come to live the Transition concretely and experience life as a collective.

Types of volunteering

Short-term volunteer: immersion in the eco-location Do you have time and desire to discover the Transition Campus and its ecosystem? With us, contribute to the reception on site and live the Transition on the eco-location! Regular punctual volunteer Do you have specific skills or desires and some time to devote to a meaningful project? Embark with us in adventure! Targeted Volunteer: a dedicated and regular mission Do you have specific skills that you want to put at the service of Transition and are available 1 day a week? Bring your stone to the building!

For more information, contact Catherine Fevrier

Coordinator Volunteer

Terms and Conditions

- Planning a volunteer day at the Transition Campus
Monday to Friday : Volunteers are invited to participate in the various projects between 9.30am-12.30am and 2pm-5pm
Saturday to Sunday : Volunteers can enjoy the place in a more informal context
- Duration of stay for short-term volunteering To ensure a good integration into the eco-location and to better understand the missions and the operation of the Campus, we recommend to volunteers a duration of stay of 3-6 weeks , flexible according to the availability of each and the places available on the eco-location. New volunteers are preferred Monday morning from 9.30 a.m. to 12.30 p.m. and departures are preferably on weekends. - Free and conscious participation in lodging and shelter The cost of meals and accommodation for the association is about 12 euros per day per person. The lodging and the cover are considered by law as benefits in kind. However, the case law on volunteering in France stipulates that the act of volunteering must be carried out without consideration, i.e. without any benefit in kind. The association is therefore obliged to charge the volunteers for their lodging and shelter. However, we also know the diversity of short-term volunteer situations that are interested in the Campus. We therefore propose a free and conscious participation without minimum for lodging and covering.

Immersion in the eco-place

As a volunteer, you will share the daily lives of members of the eco-location. Its purpose is to show how we can live happy sobriety and ecological transition in practice. A unique opportunity to :
Experience life in the eco-location
Share the daily lives of the inhabitants
Trying to live in a community and understanding how a life works as a collective
Inspire and discover everyday gestures to change lifestyle
Mutualize and live happy sobriety to fight overconsumption
Participate in activities and strengthen solidarity in Transition
Initiate workshops and friendly times: improvisation theatre, ball folk, frescoes, collaborative games
Meet and exchange with the audiences welcomed in training

Volunteer stories My name is Mathys Libeau, I am 19 years old, and I am a student in a training where I dedicate my projects to the ecological and social transition for a greater proximity to the human and non-human living. What my three weeks of volunteering on campus this summer brought me this summer is first and foremost meetings, exchanges, friendships... that will permeate my way of being, doing and living. Long live the community and the low-tech Hey! One of my contributions to the campus was the preparation of meals that I enjoyed very much, especially when you can take care of the soil and vegetables of the vegetable garden before they arrive in the kitchen. I was fascinated by how to produce the closest! Who knows, it may inspire me to maintain my garden... Thanks to my visit to Forges, I am leaving with inspiration and enthusiasm in Orléans to continue my training. I leave a lot of good memories here which, perhaps one day, will continue to be written...
Mathys Libeau
Design student - summer volunteer 2024
