January 2026 : The beginnings

Following their meeting, Campus and VoisinMalin partnered to
bring together to the Foundation of Urban Solidarity on
project « Warmly Vôtres : Acting Together against Precarity
Energy", a research-action approach with local
Priority neighbourhoods of the City, the QPV. In these neighborhoods,
the effects of climate change and energy insecurity
combine - old collective housing, overheats in summer, cold
persistent in the winter, collective heating little controllable. To
Évry-Courcouronnes (historic settlement town of VoisinMalin) and
Montereau, more than 70% of the inhabitants say they are too hot in summer
62% too cold in winter, well above average
National. The Malin Neighbours, trained in listening and
local mobilization, will be at the heart of the co-construction process,
Valuing knowledge of use, experience and local skills. The
approach aims to strengthen household power and document
social benefits of energy sobriety, while creating a
space for dialogue with donors. The project, winner of €80k of the
foundation of urban solidarity and 15k€ of the endowment fund
Qualitel, will go up for two and a half years. It started at the end of January by
a respective immersion phase: each party will discover the
the other's lifestyles (at the Transition Campus and in the QPV of the
project - Évry-Courcouronnes and the Surville district in Montereau-• He
continue with a 1-year experiment with the Malinian Neighbours and
will be followed by a dissemination of the teachings to locals. The project
will be evaluated in particular by Hélène L'huillier and Cécile Renouard, thanks to
indicators for measuring capacity and living well. Most curious
will be able to dive into "The People of the Frillies", Olivier Jandot and Renan Viguie ,
historians working on thermal comfort and in which
Operation Siberia (previous name given to Operation Chalheureuse at
Campus) is very quickly mentioned. Funny and rewarding !

Spring 2026 : First experiments

The Campus team went to Evry at the end of March for
a first meeting with the Malian Neighbours team involved in the project. Exchanges have turned
around the perception of the issues of summer and winter comfort and the question of
energy cost. The Neighbours were also very interested in
environmental issues. On May 13, the Campus team returned to Evry, this time for a
Immersion of a day. The aim was to understand the specific context of the two neighbourhoods and
of their inhabitants: the Canal and
The Pyramids district. They are both classified as priority areas of the city. They do
the subject of a major programme
urban renewal for the past ten years and the association VoisinMalin y
has been operating since 2010. Each visit, in the two neighborhoods with very different shapes, has
was organized with a Neighborhood Maline living in the neighborhood and the
Knowing like his pocket. The neighbourhood
the Canal is mainly composed of small buildings of 3 floors, when the
Pyramids consists of large buildings in the shape of... pyramids. Of the 9 Neighbours who will be involved in the project, 5 live in the
Canal area and the Pyramids area.

Team
also exchanged with an employee
the Local Energy and Climate Agency of South Paris (ALEC). This
agency was formed on the "SlowHeat" approach, a few weeks ago,
which is very close to what was experienced at the Campus via Operation
It's warm. Partnerships could emerge from these
exchanges...

The day ended with participation in a door-to-door duo or trio session with a Neighborhood with a campaign on a whole other subject. This practice is at the heart of the actions of the Association Voisin Malins and
the Campus team is very happy to have been able to participate to better understand
part of the work of the Neighbors. Finally, on the weekend of 23 and 24 May, the Voisin Malin team came to immerse themselves at the Campus! On the programme: visit of the places, exchange on the elements of thermal comfort, sharing on ways to fight against the weather and cold at different times and geographical locations, not to mention user-friendly and inter-knowledge. The weekend was very rich, both for the Campus team and for the VoisinMalin team. Some sharing of the closing time: "To link the environment with thermal comfort is a citizen's commitment; It's carrying a message for our planet." "I thought it was great what happened this weekend, it gives me a lot of hope and confidence for the continuation of the project."
